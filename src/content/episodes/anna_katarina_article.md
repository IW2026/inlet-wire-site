# The Life Inside *While I Was Dreaming*

Anna Katarina’s new record *While I Was Dreaming* sits at the centre of this Inlet Wire episode, but the conversation around it goes a bit deeper than just how the songs were made.

She talks about the title as a reflection on life happening while you are busy making plans, and that idea really gives the episode its shape. What comes through is not just the record itself, but everything around it — the expectations she had when she was younger, the reality of building a music career over time, and the moments in life that started to matter just as much as the big dream.

There is a really honest part of this episode where Anna talks about pressure too. Pressure to release, pressure to be heard, pressure to make something that lands fast. She does not overstate it, which is part of what makes that part worth hearing. It gives a clearer picture of where her head is at, and what she is trying to protect in her songwriting.

The episode also gets into how her process has changed. There is a good story in here about collaboration, about writing from a different angle, and about learning that sometimes the work only moves once you stop waiting for the perfect moment. That part says a lot about where she is at now as a writer.

And then there is the BC part of the conversation, which adds another layer. Anna talks about spending time in Nashville, then realizing she needed a life outside the music industry too — something fuller, quieter, and more grounding. That thread runs through the episode in a subtle way.

So if you want a better sense of where *While I Was Dreaming* is coming from, this episode gives you that. Not just the album pitch, but the thinking and lived-in feeling behind it.
